Travelling to India for the first time? This guide covers the practical things — visas, flights, where to stay and eat, renting an outfit, and what to see while you’re here. Specific recommendations and booking links will be added as we confirm them.

Visa & Entry

Most visitors need a visa to enter India. The simplest option for tourists is the e-Visa, applied for online before you travel at the official portal (indianvisaonline.gov.in). Apply at least 1–2 weeks ahead. Your passport should be valid for at least six months from your date of arrival and have two blank pages. Always check the current requirements for your nationality.

Flights & Getting to Jaipur

Jaipur’s airport (JAI) has limited direct international routes, so most guests fly into Delhi (DEL) or Mumbai (BOM) and take a short domestic connection to Jaipur. Carriers serving India include Air India, IndiGo and Vistara domestically, and Emirates, Qatar Airways, Lufthansa, British Airways and Air France internationally. Delhi to Jaipur is also an easy 4–5 hour drive or train if you prefer to land once.

Where to Stay in Jaipur

Jaipur has everything from heritage palace hotels to boutique guesthouses. Convenient areas include Civil Lines and C-Scheme (central, leafy), and around Amer for views and heritage stays. Renting & Buying Outfits

You don’t need to buy traditional wear — renting is easy and popular. Jaipur and Delhi have many shops and online services renting sarees, lehengas, sherwanis and kurtas, often with delivery to your hotel and help with draping. If you’d like to buy, Jaipur is famous for block-printed and bandhani fabrics.

Where to Eat

Jaipur is a paradise for vegetarian food. Try Rajasthani thalis (dal-baati-churma), street snacks like pyaaz kachori and ghewar, and lassi in a clay cup. Well-known spots include Laxmi Misthan Bhandar (LMB), Rawat Mishthan Bhandar and Chokhi Dhani for a village-style cultural dinner. Tap water isn’t safe to drink — stick to bottled or filtered water.

What to See

If you have spare days, Jaipur’s highlights include the Amber Fort, City Palace, Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), the Jantar Mantar observatory, and Nahargarh Fort for sunset views. For shopping, head to Johari Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar for jewellery, textiles and souvenirs. Agra (the Taj Mahal) is around 4–5 hours away if you want to extend your trip.

Practical Tips

Money: the currency is the Indian Rupee (INR); cards work in hotels, carry cash for markets and tips. Connectivity: a local SIM or eSIM is cheap and handy. Getting around: Uber and Ola work well in the city. Weather: February is warm and sunny by day, cooler at night — bring a light layer. Plugs: 230V, type D/M sockets — bring an adapter.
